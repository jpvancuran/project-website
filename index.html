Airbus A220

I was a part of the initial launch of the Airbus A220 line in Mobile, Ala. This application controlled the way that some of the flight control systems communicated with the rest of the aircraft’s computers. This allows a seamless flow of information between aircraft systems and allows pilots to have all flight information one just one cockpit screen. My team spent three months in Mirabel, QC learning the process from our Canadian counterparts in order to bring production to the US. As a Lead Systems Installer, I managed a group of 10-15 people daily. This included distributing work, troubleshooting problems, ensuring that all work was up to standard.

Cessna Latitude

The Cessna Latitude was where I got my start in aviation manufacturing. I worked in the final assembly area where I was working on programming a control mechanism for the dorsal intake. The dorsal intake controlled the flow of air into the climate control system for the aircraft by opening and closing the valve in relation to the amount of air flowing into it. The program that I worked on would take that airflow data and determine how much to manipulate the valve in order to maintain a consistent flow into the system. This is the last station before the aircraft goes out to paint. A lot of the job included checking over things to make sure that all of the quality marks were met before it rolled out the door. This is where me being detail-oriented really came to shine.
